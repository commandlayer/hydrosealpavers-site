# HydroSeal Growth Audit — Local SEO + CRO + Brand (2026-03-13)

## OVERALL VERDICT
- **Current overall score:** 7.1/10
- **Does the site currently look like a market leader:** **No (not yet).**
- **What it already does well:** strong service-area footprint, strong trust language (licensed/insured, Trident-certified, warranty), and broad schema deployment.
- **What is holding it back most:** repeated/template-heavy location pages, weak project-level proof architecture, and inconsistent global navigation architecture.
- **Biggest risk if nothing changes:** rankings plateau while conversion rate underperforms because pages look “competent” but not dominant or distinctly premium.

## BIGGEST GROWTH CONSTRAINTS

### 1. Template-heavy local page system without enough unique proof
- **Why it matters:** Local SEO wins now depend on page-level evidence, not just geo-swapped copy.
- **What it is costing:** Lower rank durability and weaker conversion confidence at city/neighborhood level.
- **Exact fix:** Add a mandatory unique proof block per local page (one local project, one local issue/solution, one local testimonial snippet).

### 2. Proof architecture is outsourced to widgets more than owned assets
- **Why it matters:** Owned proof (case studies, before/after stories) builds trust and SEO entities better than embeds.
- **What it is costing:** Lower premium perception and weaker differentiation.
- **Exact fix:** Build “Recent Projects” index + service-linked project cards + county-level proof hubs.

### 3. Inconsistent navigation/content architecture across templates
- **Why it matters:** Inconsistency weakens crawl clarity and UX confidence.
- **What it is costing:** Internal-link leakage, potential dead-link risk, and weaker user progression.
- **Exact fix:** Standardize global nav source and remove invalid nav targets (e.g., non-existent `restoration-cleaning`).
