How often should pavers be sealed in Doctors Inlet?

Most homeowners reseal about every 2–3 years. Driveways with daily parking and full sun wear faster than shaded patios. The best results come from recoating before the sealer wears completely through.

Do you have to replace joint sand before sealing pavers?

If joint sand is low or washing out, yes. We remove deteriorated material and re-sand using Trident joint sand (ASTM-C144), compacted to the correct depth. Skipping joint restoration is a main reason pavers shift and weeds return after heavy rain.

Will sealing help with weeds, ants, and joint washout?

Sealing helps protect the surface, but long-term joint performance comes from restoring and compacting the joint sand first. Stable joints usually mean less washout, fewer weeds taking hold, and less ant tunneling. If drainage/base issues exist, those should be addressed before sealing.

Does paver sealing make surfaces slippery?

It can if the wrong product or application method is used. For walkways and pool decks, we adjust the finish and can use traction-focused options when appropriate. Slippery areas usually come from puddling or excessive buildup—controlled application prevents that.

What causes paver sealing jobs to fail in Clay County?

Most failures come from sealing damp pavers, skipping joint re-sanding, using low-grade sealers, or rushing dry times. Clay County humidity and storms expose poor prep quickly. We prioritize moisture control, joint integrity, and controlled application to help prevent haze, peeling, and premature sand loss.

When is the best time to seal pavers in Doctors Inlet?

Spring through early fall is ideal as long as the surface is fully dry and weather cooperates. If humidity is high, rain is too close, or moisture is trapped in shaded areas, we delay rather than risk a failed finish.
