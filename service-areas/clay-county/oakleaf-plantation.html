Our Paver Sealing Process (St. Johns County + Jacksonville)

Most problems come from skipped prep. We do the work that actually determines the outcome: sand removal, treatment, deep cleaning, re-sanding, and controlled sealer application.

  1. Joint prep + old sand removal: We remove failing joint material so the new sand bonds and stabilizes correctly.
  2. Efflorescence treatment (when present): If needed, we use the right chemistry for that condition before sealing.
  3. Professional cleaning: We use controlled, professional-grade solutions (including bleach when appropriate) to remove organic staining — without “burning” the surface.
  4. Full surface cleaning: Rinse, extraction, and detail work so you don’t seal in dirt, film, or residue.
  5. Re-sanding with quality joint sand: We install clean joint sand (ASTM C144) and hydro-compact for strong, crisp joints. Ask about color options.
  6. Controlled sealing: We apply premium sealer in measured coats for consistent coverage and a clean finish.
Typical schedule: 1-day in spring/summer (when conditions allow)

Paver Sealing FAQs

How long does paver sealing last in Florida?

It depends on sun exposure, drainage, traffic, and maintenance. Many homeowners plan on resealing every few years. The bigger issue is whether the prep and sand work were done correctly the first time.

Can you clean and seal pavers the same day?

Sometimes, yes — especially in spring and summer when conditions are right. If moisture, shade, or weather makes dry-time risky, we extend the process. Rushing is how you get haze, whitening, or adhesion issues.

Do you use bleach when cleaning pavers?

When appropriate, we use bleach in a controlled, professional way as part of a disciplined cleaning system — not homeowner-strength “dump and pray.” The goal is effective removal of organic growth without damaging surfaces or surrounding landscaping.

Will sealing make my pavers slippery?

Slip risk depends on the surface, finish, and conditions (especially around pools). We choose products and application methods to keep surfaces safe and usable. If a surface can’t meet our standards, we’ll tell you.
