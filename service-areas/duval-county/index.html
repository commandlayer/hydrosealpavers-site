Why Paver Sealing Matters in Duval County

Duval County pavers deal with real-world abuse: sun and UV, storm-season downpours, shaded growth, and lots of daily traffic. Sealing is about long-term protection — not a quick “shine job.”

1) UV + Heat Fade Driveways and Front Walks

Full sun hits hard in Jacksonville and surrounding neighborhoods. Over time, pavers can lose depth, look patchy, and feel “chalky.” A quality sealer helps reduce UV impact and keeps the surface looking more even.

2) Shade + Organic Runoff = Dark Spots and Slick Growth

Duval has plenty of tree canopy and shaded areas that trap moisture. That’s where algae and mildew take hold, especially on patios, side yards, and north-facing sections. Proper cleaning + sealing helps reduce absorption and makes maintenance easier.

3) Heavy Rain Washes Out Joint Sand (That’s When Problems Start)

When joints wash out, weeds and ants move in, and pavers can start shifting or rocking. This is one of the most common “why does my driveway feel uneven?” causes. A correct process includes re-sanding and stabilization so joints stay tight.

4) Pool Decks + Travertine Need a Controlled System

Around pools, slippery surfaces and uneven application are the big risks. Travertine and natural stone also require the right product and technique. The goal is a clean, premium finish that stays functional — not tacky or overdone.

Paver Sealing FAQ — Duval County

Straight answers to the questions Duval homeowners ask before sealing a driveway, patio, or pool deck.

How often should you seal pavers in Duval County?

Most homeowners reseal every 2–3 years. Full sun driveways, frequent washing, and heavy rain flow can shorten the cycle. Shaded areas may need cleaning more often, even if the sealer is still performing.

Why is my joint sand disappearing?

Heavy rain, irrigation overspray, and aggressive pressure washing can wash joints out over time. Once joints open up, weeds and ants move in and pavers can start shifting. Re-sanding and stabilization is a core part of doing this correctly.

Can you seal pavers that already have algae or dark stains?

Yes — but not by sealing over it. The surface needs proper cleaning and treatment first, otherwise staining can get trapped and look worse. Prep is what separates a premium job from a quick cosmetic job.

Will sealing make my pool deck slippery?

It can if the wrong product is used or applied too heavy. For pool areas, the right approach is controlled application and selecting a system that keeps the surface functional and comfortable.
