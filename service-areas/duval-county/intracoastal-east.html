Our process (built for humidity + storm season)

Most sealing failures are caused by moisture and heavy-handed application. We follow a consistent process that prevents haze, blotchiness, and premature sand loss.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics + grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

Do you seal pool decks differently than driveways?

Yes. Pool areas stay wetter and need more control to avoid slick spots and buildup. We focus on even coverage, clean edges, and practical traction options when appropriate. The goal is a premium finish that stays walkable when wet.

Will sealing help with sand washout after heavy rain?

It helps — if the joint work is done correctly first. We re-sand low joints and compact to proper depth, then apply sealer in a controlled way so the joints stay tighter through storm season and irrigation cycles.
