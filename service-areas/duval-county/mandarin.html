Our process (built for shade + humidity)

In Mandarin, moisture is the enemy. The fastest way to get haze and blotchiness is sealing before the surface is truly dry or applying product too heavy. We keep coats controlled and timing disciplined so the finish cures correctly.

  1. Inspection: organics, stains, drainage, joint depth
  2. Deep clean: lift algae/mildew + grime without blowing out joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after rain)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

Mandarin Paver Sealing Questions

Does sealing help with algae and mildew in shaded Mandarin areas?

It helps when it’s done correctly: clean first, rinse thoroughly, and let the surface fully dry before sealing. Sealing doesn’t “kill” algae — it helps reduce absorption and makes future cleanup easier.

Do you seal pool decks differently than driveways?

Yes. Pool areas stay wet more often and need a more controlled approach: clean edges, controlled coats, and traction-friendly options when needed. The goal is a premium finish that stays walkable when wet.

Can you re-sand the joints if they’re low or washed out?

Yes. We restore joint sand depth and compact properly before sealing when needed. In Florida, that joint work is often the difference between a job that holds up and one that starts washing out quickly.
