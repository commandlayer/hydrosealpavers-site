Most sealing failures come from moisture and shortcuts: sealing damp pavers, skipping joint restoration, or flooding the surface with product. We use dry-time discipline and controlled coats so the finish looks premium and the joints stay stable.

Jacksonville Paver Sealing Questions

How often should pavers be resealed in Jacksonville?

Most homes land around 2–3 years, but exposure is the real driver. Full-sun driveways and areas that see heavy runoff usually need maintenance sooner than shaded patios.

Why does joint sand disappear so fast in Jacksonville?

Storms, irrigation overspray, and water sheeting across sloped surfaces slowly pull sand out of the joints. Once joints open up, weeds and ants move in and pavers can start shifting. Proper re-sanding + compaction is key.

Can you remove algae, mildew, and dark staining before sealing?

Yes — but not by sealing over it. We clean first, rinse thoroughly, and let the surface truly dry before sealing. Sealing over organics is how you get permanent blotches and haze.
