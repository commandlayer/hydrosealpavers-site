RiverTown Paver Sealing Questions

Does river humidity in RiverTown affect paver lifespan?

Yes. Persistent humidity and damp cycles can accelerate fading, organic growth, and joint deterioration when pavers are left unprotected.

Can sealing help with mildew and organic buildup in RiverTown?

It helps significantly. Sealed surfaces absorb less water, so mildew and organic residue are less likely to penetrate deeply and are easier to clean.

Why do RiverTown paver joints wear out faster in some areas?

Frequent moisture, runoff, and regular use can loosen or wash out joint sand over time, especially on driveways and sloped areas.

Do you re-sand before sealing in RiverTown?

Yes. Joint re-sanding is a core part of the process and is completed before sealing so pavers have better structural lock and durability. See our sand options to match performance and finish preferences.

How long should we wait after sealing before using pavers?

Most surfaces become touch-dry in a few hours, but we usually recommend 24 hours before foot traffic and 24–48 hours before vehicles.

Can faded RiverTown pavers be restored without replacement?

In many cases, yes. If the paver field is structurally sound, deep cleaning, re-sanding, and sealing can restore appearance and extend service life.
