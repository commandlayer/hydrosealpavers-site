Our process (built to avoid haze + slick spots)

Most problems come from moisture and shortcuts: sealing damp pavers, skipping joint restoration, or over-applying product. We use dry-time discipline and controlled passes so the finish looks premium and the joints stay stable.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics + grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

SilverLeaf Paver Sealing Questions

Can you seal pavers if sprinklers run every morning?

Yes, but irrigation timing matters. We need a true dry window before sealing. We’ll coordinate to pause sprinklers so the pavers dry fully and the sealer cures correctly.

Will sealing make my pool deck too slick?

It can if the wrong finish is used or if sealer is over-applied. We apply in controlled passes and can recommend slip-resistance options for walking areas when appropriate.

Do you remove old sealer or “patchy” coating first?

If there’s an existing coating, we’ll evaluate it first. Some surfaces need additional prep to avoid uneven results. We’ll tell you what’s required to get a clean, uniform finish.
