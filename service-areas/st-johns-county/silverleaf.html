SilverLeaf Paver Sealing Questions

How soon should new pavers be sealed in SilverLeaf?

For many newer installations, sealing is best once the surface is fully cured and dry, typically within the first year or two depending on install conditions. Waiting too long can allow early sand loss and staining to start.

Do SilverLeaf pavers that still look new really need sealing?

Yes. New-looking pavers can still absorb water, lose joint sand, and fade under Florida sun. SilverLeaf paver sealing is most valuable before wear is obvious.

Do you re-sand before sealing in SilverLeaf?

Absolutely. We remove weak material, install kiln-dried joint sand, and compact it before sealing so the surface has better stability and longer-lasting performance. See our sand options to match performance and finish preferences.

Can sealing help with washout and staining on SilverLeaf driveways and patios?

Yes. Proper prep and sealing reduce water penetration and help limit deep staining, while stabilized joints are less vulnerable to washout during heavy rain.

How long does paver sealer need to cure in SilverLeaf?

Most projects are dry to the touch within a few hours, but we typically recommend about 24 hours before foot traffic and 24–48 hours before vehicles, based on weather conditions.

Why does early paver sealing matter in St. Johns County communities like SilverLeaf?

Early protection helps preserve color, joint integrity, and surface condition before UV exposure, rainfall, and organic growth create more expensive restoration needs later.
