Our process (built to avoid the common failures)

The fastest way to ruin pavers is sealing too soon or laying product too thick. We follow a repeatable process that prevents haze, blotchiness, and premature sand loss — especially in Florida humidity.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics + grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

Palencia Paver Sealing Questions

Do you seal pavers in Palencia year-round?

Most of the year, yes. The limiting factor is moisture: rain forecasts, surface dampness, and humidity. If conditions aren’t right, we’ll adjust timing so the sealer cures correctly.

Will sealing stop weeds and ants in the joints?

Sealing helps by stabilizing the joint sand and reducing washout, which makes it harder for weeds to take hold. It’s not magic, but with proper joint restoration, it can significantly reduce recurring growth and ant activity.

Can you fix low joints and sand washout before sealing?

Yes. We re-sand the joints when needed and compact to the proper depth. In Florida storms, that joint work is often the difference between a job that holds up and one that fails early.
