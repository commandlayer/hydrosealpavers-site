Our Professional 6 Step Process

Each project follows the same proven workflow, adapted for local driveway, patio, and pool-deck conditions.

Crosswater Paver Sealing Questions

How long should we wait before using pavers after sealing in Crosswater?

Most surfaces are dry to the touch in about 1–2 hours in good weather. We usually recommend 24 hours before normal foot traffic and 24–48 hours before vehicles or heavy furniture so the sealer cures properly in Florida humidity.

Does sealing help control mildew and algae in Crosswater?

Yes. Sealing reduces water absorption so organic growth is easier to clean and less likely to stain deeply. In Crosswater, routine rinsing and periodic cleaning still matter, but sealed pavers stay more manageable between services.

Do you re-sand joints before sealing pavers in Crosswater?

Yes. We remove weak joint material, install kiln-dried sand, and compact joints before sealing. Proper joint sand lockup supports surface stability and helps reduce washout during storms.

Will sealed pool deck pavers feel slippery in Crosswater?

A properly applied sealer should improve appearance without creating an unsafe film. We control coverage rates and finish level for traffic areas so surfaces remain predictable under wet conditions.

How often should pavers be resealed in Crosswater?

Most residential pavers in Crosswater benefit from resealing about every 2–3 years, depending on sun exposure, shade, traffic, and drainage. We recommend inspection before setting a timeline.

Can older Crosswater pavers be restored instead of replaced?

In many cases, yes. Deep cleaning, joint stabilization, and professional sealing can restore appearance and function without full replacement when the base and edge restraints are still sound.
