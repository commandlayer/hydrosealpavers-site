What we see most in Nocatee (and how we handle it)

Nocatee pavers take a beating from sun, storms, sprinklers, and daily traffic. Here’s what shows up most often — and how we fix it the right way.

Faded / uneven color

We deep-clean, reset joints, and apply a controlled coat to restore a clean, uniform finish — not blotchy, not streaky.

Joint sand loss

Rain + sprinklers + pressure washing can blow out joints. We re-sand with clean kiln-dried sand and compact it properly before sealing.

Organic staining

Shade + humidity = algae/mildew/tannin stains. We treat correctly, rinse thoroughly, and only seal when the surface is actually ready.

Our standard: clean, even, premium — not “wet/oily,” not sticky, not hazy. If the weather window isn’t right, we reschedule. Quality beats rushing.

Nocatee Paver Sealing FAQs

How often should pavers be sealed in Nocatee?

Most homeowners reseal every 2–3 years depending on sun exposure, traffic, and drainage. High-sun driveways often need it sooner.

Does sealer make pavers stain-proof?

No. It helps resist absorption and makes cleanup easier, but spills still need to be handled quickly.

Will sealing make pavers look “wet” or slippery?

Finish depends on product choice. We aim for a clean, premium look — not over-glossy — and avoid heavy build-up that can create slick spots.
