What we see most in Nocatee (and how we handle it)

Faded / uneven color

Sun + weather + traffic can wash out color and make surfaces look patchy. We deep-clean, reset joints, and apply a controlled coat to restore a clean, uniform finish.

Joint sand loss

Rain, sprinklers, and pressure washing can blow out joints. We re-sand with clean kiln-dried joint sand and compact it properly before sealing.

Organic staining

Shade, mulch beds, and humidity drive algae/mildew/tannin staining. We treat the surface correctly, rinse thoroughly, and seal once it’s actually ready.

Goal: clean, even, premium — not “wet and oily,” not streaky, not sticky. If the weather window isn’t right, we reschedule. Quality beats rushing.

Nocatee Paver Sealing FAQs

How often should pavers be sealed in Nocatee?
Most homeowners reseal every 2–3 years depending on sun exposure, traffic, and drainage. Pool decks and high-sun driveways usually need it sooner.
Does sealer make pavers stain-proof?
No. It helps resist absorption and makes cleanup easier, but spills still need to be handled quickly.
Will sealing make my pavers look “wet” or slippery?
It depends on product choice and finish. We aim for a clean, premium look — not over-glossy — and we avoid heavy build-up that creates slick spots.
