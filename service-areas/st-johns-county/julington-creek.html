Our process (built to avoid the common failures)

The fastest way to ruin pavers is sealing too soon or laying product too thick. We follow a repeatable process that prevents haze, blotchiness, and premature sand loss — especially in Florida humidity.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics + grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

Julington Creek Paver Sealing Questions

Do you serve Julington Creek for paver sealing?

Yes. We service Julington Creek for paver sealing on driveways, patios, walkways, and pool decks. We’ll recommend the right prep based on sun exposure, staining, and joint condition.

Is it possible to seal in humid weather?

Yes—if the pavers are fully dry and conditions are right. Humidity can slow cure time, so we follow dry-time discipline and don’t seal damp surfaces.

Do you re-sand the joints before sealing?

When joints are low or washed out, yes. Proper joint re-sanding and compaction helps stabilize the surface and reduces sand loss after storms and irrigation.
