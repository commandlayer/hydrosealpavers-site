What sealing should do for your Ponte Vedra pavers

A premium job isn’t about “making it shiny.” It’s about stability, uniformity, and making the surface easier to keep clean — without creating blotches or tacky areas.

  • Richer color + more even finish
  • Joint sand stabilization (less washout)
  • Helps resist stains from grime + organics
  • Reduced weed growth in joints
  • Easier rinsing and maintenance after storms

Our process (the part that matters)

Most “paver sealing” failures come from shortcuts: sealing damp pavers, skipping joint restoration, or flooding the surface with product. We run a consistent process so the finish looks premium and the joints stay stable.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, problem stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics and grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical in Florida storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling
