Our process (built for Florida humidity)

The fastest way to ruin pavers is sealing too soon or laying product too thick. We follow a repeatable process that prevents haze, blotchiness, and premature sand loss — especially when humidity and shade slow drying.

  1. Inspection: old coatings, stains, drainage, joint condition
  2. Deep clean: lift organics + grime without destroying joints
  3. Joint re-sand: proper depth + compaction (critical after storms)
  4. Dry-time discipline: we don’t seal damp pavers — period
  5. Controlled seal: even passes, clean edges, no puddling

Shearwater Paver Sealing Questions

Can you seal pavers in Shearwater even if parts stay shaded?

Yes, but shade changes drying and cure time. We check moisture and don’t seal damp areas. If a section isn’t ready, we adjust timing so the finish cures correctly and doesn’t haze.

Will sealing help with joint sand washout from rain and irrigation?

Yes—if the joints are restored properly first. Re-sanding to the right depth and compacting before sealing helps stabilize the joints and reduces washout after storms and sprinkler cycles.

Do you offer slip-resistance options for pool decks and walkways?

When it makes sense, yes. Slip risk depends on the surface, finish, and application thickness. We apply sealer in controlled passes and can recommend traction options for walking surfaces.
