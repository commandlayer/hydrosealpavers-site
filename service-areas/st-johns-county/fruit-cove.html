Fruit Cove Paver Sealing Questions

How do I know if my Fruit Cove pavers should be restored instead of replaced?

If pavers are structurally sound but look faded, stained, or have weak joints, restoration is usually the better first option before considering full replacement.

What are signs my pavers need re-sanding?

Visible joint loss, loose-feeling pavers, weed growth between joints, and washout after rain are all common signs that re-sanding is needed. See our sand options to match performance and finish preferences.

Can older Fruit Cove pavers look good again without replacement?

Often, yes. Professional cleaning, joint stabilization, and sealing can significantly improve appearance and extend service life.

Why is failing joint sand a serious issue?

Joint sand helps lock pavers together. When it breaks down, pavers can shift, edges can weaken, and water can penetrate more easily.

How long does sealer cure time take in Fruit Cove?

Most surfaces are touch-dry in a few hours. We usually recommend 24 hours before foot traffic and 24–48 hours before vehicles.

Is paver restoration in Fruit Cove usually cost-effective?

Yes. When the underlying structure is still solid, restoration is typically far more cost-effective than tear-out and replacement.
