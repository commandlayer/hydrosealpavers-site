Our Professional 6 Step Process

Coastal Oaks surfaces often require careful prep across larger, more visible hardscapes. Our process is built to keep finish quality consistent from driveway aprons to pool deck edges and courtyard transitions.

  1. Inspection: review surface condition, drainage, shade exposure, joint wear, and large-area visibility
  2. Clean: use controlled cleaning and targeted treatment to remove buildup, grime, and organic staining
  3. Re-sand: install and compact kiln-dried joint sand where joints have weakened or washed out; sand color options are available when appropriate
  4. Dry & verify: confirm moisture levels and weather conditions before sealing
  5. Seal: apply even coats with controlled finish level and clean transitions across prominent outdoor surfaces
  6. Final walkthrough: review cure time, care guidance, and long-term maintenance recommendations

Coastal Oaks Paver Sealing Questions

What makes paver sealing in Coastal Oaks different from a standard neighborhood job?

Coastal Oaks homes often have larger, more visible paver areas where prep quality and finish consistency matter more. The process has to be controlled from start to finish.

Do larger driveways and courtyard entries need different prep before sealing?

Usually yes. Larger surfaces require more detailed cleaning, moisture checks, and transition control to keep the finish uniform.

Do you re-sand joints before sealing in Coastal Oaks?

Yes. We replace and compact weak or missing joint sand before sealing to support long-term joint performance.

Will sealing help keep premium paver surfaces looking cleaner longer?

Yes. Proper sealing helps reduce rapid absorption and makes ongoing cleaning easier while preserving a more even appearance.

How long should we stay off the surface after sealing?

Most surfaces can handle light foot traffic after about 24 hours, while vehicles should generally wait up to 48 hours depending on conditions.

How often should pavers in Coastal Oaks be resealed?

Most pavers are resealed every 2–3 years, based on sun exposure, traffic levels, and overall wear. For added long-term confidence, review our warranty coverage.
