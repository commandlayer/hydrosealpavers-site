Driveways take the most abuse—sun, rain, tires, and sand loss. A proper seal is not “spray and pray.” The prep is what makes it look premium and last.

What Driveway Sealing Does

Restores color, helps lock joint sand in place, and adds a protective barrier against stains and weather. You get cleaner edges, fewer weeds, and a driveway that looks “finished.”

  • Color enhancement + uniform finish
  • Joint sand stabilization (less washout)
  • Helps resist oil, rust, and dirt staining
  • Reduced weed growth in joints

Our Process (The Part That Matters)

Anyone can spray sealer. The difference is prep + re-sanding + an even application. That’s what prevents blotchy results and early failure.

  1. Inspection: surface condition, old coatings, problem spots
  2. Clean: correct chemistry + rinse to lift organics and grime
  3. Re-sand: refill joints (polymeric where appropriate)
  4. Seal: even coat, controlled application, clean edges
  5. Final walkthrough: care notes + drying time guidance

Driveway Sealing Questions

How long before I can park on my driveway after sealing?

Most driveways need at least 24 hours before vehicle traffic, depending on humidity, shade, and product used. We’ll give you a clear “park time” on the day of service so you don’t risk tire marks or tracking.

Will sealing help prevent tire marks, oil spots, and rust stains?

Sealing helps reduce absorption and makes cleanup easier, especially for oil and organic grime. It won’t make pavers “stain-proof,” but it buys you time and improves how the surface handles everyday driveway wear.

Do you re-sand the joints on driveways (and do you use polymeric sand)?

Yes. Driveways lose joint sand fast from tires and rainfall. We remove failing joint material and re-sand using Trident ASTM-C144 joint sand. If polymeric is appropriate for your joints and conditions, we’ll recommend it — the goal is stability, clean joints, and long-term performance.

Can you clean and seal my driveway the same day?

Often, yes — especially in spring and summer when conditions are right. If the pavers hold moisture, there’s shade, or rain is nearby, we extend dry time. Rushing a driveway seal is how you get whitening, tacky spots, and failures.

Does driveway sealing make pavers slippery?

Driveways are usually not a traction problem, but application matters. We apply evenly, avoid over-application, and choose the right finish for your surface so it looks clean and performs without feeling slick.

What if my driveway has sinking pavers, low spots, or drainage issues?

We inspect for rocking, sinking, and drainage problems and tell you the right fix first. Sealing protects the surface and helps stabilize joints — it does not correct base or structural issues. If repairs are needed, we’ll be upfront before sealing begins.
