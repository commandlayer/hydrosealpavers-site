Why paver sealing is worth it

The real value isn’t “shine.” It’s protection, stability, and easier maintenance — when the job is done correctly.

Locks in joint sand

Stabilized joints are the difference between pavers that stay tight and pavers that slowly loosen over time. When joints are properly cleaned out, filled to the correct depth, and compacted, you reduce washout, weed growth, ant tunneling, and shifting — and you keep the whole surface feeling solid underfoot (and under tires).

Joint stability
Less washout
Less weeds/ants
Tighter, cleaner joints
What most companies skip: joint prep + correct sand depth + proper compaction. That’s what prevents movement and callbacks — not “extra shiny sealer.”

Helps resist stains

A quality sealer creates a protective barrier that helps reduce absorption from oil, dirt, organic staining, and everyday grime. That doesn’t mean “stain-proof” — it means you get more time to clean spills before they soak in, and routine maintenance becomes easier and more predictable.

Easier cleanup
Helps reduce absorption
Protects color
Less grime buildup
Reality: the faster a spill is handled, the better the result. We’ll give you simple care guidance so you don’t accidentally damage the finish trying to clean it.

Restores curb appeal

Proper cleaning + fresh sand + a controlled sealer application can bring back depth, uniformity, and that “maintained” look. The goal isn’t to make it look wet or overdone — it’s to make the surface look clean, even, and premium, without streaking, blotching, or tacky spots.

Richer color
Uniform finish
Cleaner look
Less faded appearance
This is where pros show: consistent coverage, clean edges, and no puddling. If a surface can’t meet our standards (or the weather window isn’t right), we reschedule.
Reality check: sealing does not fix base/structural issues. If pavers are sinking, rocking, or holding water, we’ll tell you upfront what needs to be corrected (and what sealing can and cannot do).

Our step-by-step paver sealing process

This is the difference between a job that lasts and one that fails. Most issues come from rushing prep or sealing too soon.

1) Inspection & surface evaluation

We review condition, stains, drainage/standing water, joint condition, and any problem areas. We set expectations before we start.

  • Identify shifting/sinking (base issues)
  • Spot problem stains and organic growth
  • Confirm sealer goals (natural vs enhanced look)

2) Deep cleaning (the right way)

Cleaning removes dirt, organic growth, and surface contamination so the sealer bonds correctly and looks uniform.

  • Controlled cleaning to protect joints
  • Targeted treatment where needed
  • Thorough rinse and surface check

3) Joint prep & sanding

We restore joints and add the correct sand to help stabilize the pavers. This is where long-term performance comes from.

  • Remove debris from joints
  • Refill to proper depth
  • Compact and level the joints

4) Dry time (non-negotiable)

Sealer and moisture don’t mix. If conditions aren’t right, we wait. Rushing this step is the #1 reason sealers fail.

  • Surface dryness check
  • Weather window planning
  • Protecting against surprise rain

5) Professional sealer application

We apply sealer in controlled passes and manage edges, overlap, and coverage to avoid puddling, streaking, and tacky spots.

  • Even coverage
  • Clean borders/edges
  • Correct coat strategy for the surface

6) Final walkthrough + care instructions

We verify coverage, address any small touch-ups, and give clear cure-time and maintenance guidance so you don’t accidentally damage the finish.

  • Traffic timing guidance
  • Cleaning do’s and don’ts
  • Maintenance plan for long-term results
What we will NOT do: seal wet pavers, seal right before rain, or “spray and pray.” Those shortcuts create failures and callbacks.

How to maintain sealed pavers

The fastest way to ruin a fresh seal is aggressive cleaning or harsh chemicals. Follow this and your surface stays cleaner longer.

Do

  • Rinse periodically to remove dust and debris
  • Use mild soap and a gentle brush for small spots
  • Address spills quickly (especially oil)
  • Call us if you’re unsure how to clean a stain

Don’t

  • Pressure wash sealed pavers aggressively
  • Use acids, strong degreasers, or harsh cleaners
  • Drag heavy furniture across the surface
  • Assume “more product” is better — over-application causes problems
Want the easiest maintenance plan? Ask about periodic cleaning and touch-up scheduling for high-traffic areas.
