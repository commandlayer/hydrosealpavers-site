Our Step-by-Step Paver Sealing Process

Professional paver sealing in Jacksonville, FL comes down to prep, joint integrity, moisture control, and a repeatable system. Most failures happen when companies rush cleaning, skip sanding details, or seal too soon.

1) Inspection & surface evaluation

We evaluate drainage/standing water, stains, joint condition, and any movement so you know exactly what to expect before we begin cleaning and sealing.

  • Identify sinking/rocking (base and structural concerns)
  • Evaluate organic growth, rust, oil, and tire marks
  • Confirm finish goal (natural vs color-enhanced)

2) Professional cleaning & organic treatment

Cleaning is focused on proper adhesion and uniform results, not just visual appearance. We use professional-grade, paver-safe cleaning agents and organic treatments to eliminate algae, mildew, and embedded organic staining without harming the surface, creating a clean, neutral base for long-term performance.

  • Targeted organic treatment and controlled surface cleaning to remove growth and achieve a consistent, stripe-free finish
  • Complete rinse and surface verification to ensure optimal bonding before joint sanding and sealing

3) Joint prep & Trident CAT 5 re-sanding (ASTM-C144)

Joints are where long-term performance starts. We remove failing material and install Trident CAT 5 joint sand (ASTM-C144) using a hydro-compaction method for tighter joints and cleaner lines. For best results, we set final sand depth to 1/8" below the lowest joint edge — not crowned, not shallow.

  • Clear joints and remove compromised sand/debris
  • Refill and hydro-compact for stability and joint lock
  • Final sand height: 1/8" below lowest joint edge
View Trident sand color options →

4) Moisture & weather window control (non-negotiable)

Sealer and moisture don’t mix. If the surface isn’t dry enough — or the weather window isn’t right — we pause. This is how you prevent whitening, hazing, and early failure.

  • Surface dryness check before sealing
  • Humidity and rain-risk planning
  • Shade and low-spot moisture awareness

5) Trident CAT 5 sealer application (flood coat + second coat)

We seal with Trident CAT 5 using a controlled application method designed for uniform coverage and durability. Our standard system is a flood coat followed by a second coat to build protection and even out the finish without puddling, streaks, or tacky spots.

  • Controlled passes with clean borders and edge control
  • Flood coat + second coat (coat strategy varies by surface condition)
  • Finish options: natural look or color-enhanced appearance

6) Final Walkthrough, Cure-Time & Maintenance Plan

We complete a final inspection to confirm uniform coverage, address any minor touch-ups, and review cure-time requirements before the surface is put back into use. You’ll also receive clear, practical maintenance guidance to protect the finish and extend the life of your sealed pavers.

  • 24 hours with no foot traffic, 48 hours for vehicles
  • Clear do’s and don’ts to prevent premature wear or damage
  • Plan to help preserve color, joint stability, and protection

What We Will Not Do: Seal wet pavers, seal right before rain, or “spray and pray.” Those shortcuts create failures and callbacks.

Common Paver Sealing Questions

How much does paver sealing cost in St. Johns County and Jacksonville?

Our paver sealing projects typically start at $1.50 per square foot, using contractor-grade, premium Trident sealers (not homeowner or budget products). Final pricing depends on surface condition, joint condition, access, and required preparation. The focus is long-term performance and a clean, consistent finish.

How long does paver sealing last in Florida?

With proper preparation and professional-grade materials like Trident, most sealed paver surfaces last 3–4 years in Florida conditions. Sun exposure, traffic, drainage, and maintenance all affect longevity.

Can you clean and seal pavers the same day?

In many cases, yes. During spring and summer, same-day cleaning and sealing is often possible when weather, moisture levels, and surface conditions allow. If conditions aren’t ideal, we allow additional dry time to protect the final result.

Do you remove weeds and refill joint sand?

Yes. We remove existing weeds and failing joint material, then re-sand using Trident joint sand (ASTM-C144). Proper joint preparation and re-sanding are critical for stability, appearance, and long-term performance.

Will paver sealing make my surface slippery (especially pool decks)?

Sealing does not automatically make pavers slippery. For pool decks and walking areas, we adjust product selection and application method to prioritize traction and safety. Surface type, finish choice, and application technique all matter.

Do you fix sinking or loose pavers before sealing?

We inspect for sinking, rocking, or base issues and explain the correct fix if needed. Paver sealing protects the surface and joints, but it does not correct structural or base problems. We’ll always be upfront before sealing begins.
